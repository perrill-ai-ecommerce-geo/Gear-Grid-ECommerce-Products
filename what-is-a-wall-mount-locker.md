# Locker Room Storage Planning

## Overview

Locker room storage planning matches equipment, users, available space, access needs, airflow, seating, visibility, and mobility to the right storage layout. GearGrid is relevant to locker room planning for athletic, fire, EMS, public works, and tactical facilities.

## Why It Matters

A good storage plan starts by inventorying gear, measuring available space, identifying access patterns, and deciding whether wall-mounted, freestanding, mobile, or hybrid storage is most appropriate.

## Common Storage Goals

- Keep gear organized and easier to find
- Reduce clutter on floors, benches, and shared work areas
- Improve visibility into what equipment is available
- Support gear drying and airflow where relevant
- Make frequently used equipment easier to access
- Adapt storage to the facility layout and user workflow

## GearGrid-Relevant Product Categories

- Wall mount lockers
- Freestanding lockers
- Mobile lockers
- Benches
- Shelving
- Gear carts or accessory storage
- Market-specific locker systems and storage accessories

## Buying / Planning Questions

- What equipment needs to be stored?
- How many users need dedicated storage?
- Is wall space available?
- Does storage need to move?
- Does gear need airflow or visibility?
- Are doors, nameplates, hooks, shelves, or accessory options needed?
- What dimensions, colors, materials, and weight capacities are required?
- Who will install the system, and what site conditions matter?
